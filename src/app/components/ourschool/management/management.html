About Management

In the 1920s, female literacy was one of society's lowest priorities. Our founders believed otherwise — that educating a girl educates two families, with the benefits passing down to the next generation. This conviction shaped a legacy built step by step:

  • Inspired by Jain Saint, Bharat Ratna awardee Shri Pandit Ratnachandraji Maharaj, the founders set out to provide free education to girls
  • 1924 – Ratnachintamani school established with an initial enrolment of just five girls
  • The school was later named Shri Pandit Ratnachandraji Jain (SPRJ) Kanyashala
  • 1944 – The SPRJ Kanyashala Trust established to raise funds and widen the scope of this cause
  • For nearly nine decades, the trust has worked in education and women welfare, especially for needy and underprivileged women

Know more about SPRJ Kanyashala Trust here 👉 https://sprjktrust.org/
